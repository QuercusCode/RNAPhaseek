## 5. Blunt honest assessment

**Did this deep sweep find materially more than the prior haul? Modestly yes on count, but with sharply diminishing returns and heavy mechanistic redundancy. The protein-free RNA-only-LLPS literature is now effectively near-exhausted for genuinely independent signal.**

What is genuinely new and worth the effort:
- **A handful of high-value, sequence-resolved, non-yeast wet-lab sources with matched negatives that were NOT previously catalogued:** the CZ/2CZ aptamer hydrogel (only true directed-evolution RNA self-assembler, sequences in-hand), the two Williams/Bevilacqua G4 flanking/G-tract papers (clean structure-matched pos/neg matrices), the 2′-OH chemistry-negative paper, and the bacterial-expression nanostar paper (Ng/Takinoue). These ~5–6 sources are the real payoff and directly attack the corpus's two weaknesses (yeast dominance, structure-specificity hard negatives).
- **Several enrichment-of-known entries with load-bearing matched negatives** (Raguseo motif panel, Poudyal M1/M2, Roschdi (GU) ladder, Zhang/Ding SHR) that materially improve hard-negative coverage without being new sources.

What is NOT new in any meaningful sense:
- **The entire computational CAG/CUG/G4C2 cluster** (Nguyen, Maity ×2, Kimchi, Li&Chen, Ramachandran, Wadsworth-2′OH, Schmoll) is the same repeat-RNA physics re-derived across methods. After de-leak/dedup these collapse to the already-known (CAG)ₙ/(CUG)ₙ family plus *feature signals* (parity, salt, 2′-OH, m6A, base hierarchy G>A>C>U) — useful as features, near-worthless as independent sequences.
- **The Bellini/Clark/Fraccia RNA-liquid-crystal lineage** (Zanchetta 2008, Todisco 2018, Bellini 2009, Mukherjee theory, plus DNA GTAC/TGB/nanostar physics) is mechanistically real and protein-free, but (a) much of it is DNA, (b) the RNA sequences are paywalled in SIs, (c) it is lyotropic LC ordering, a distinct regime from intracellular RNA-LLPS — low yield, boundary relevance.
- **The biggest finding is a negative one:** the targeted gaps — true extremophile/thermophile protein-free RNA-LLPS, non-disease structured-RNA self-condensation, plant/fungal/invertebrate natural-mRNA protein-free LLPS — **still have essentially no dedicated wet-lab data.** Lyu 2025 (16S rRNA G4), retrozymes, and viroids are candidate-sequence-only; the Drosophila germ-granule and C. elegans P-granule systems are protein-dependent. The strict non-yeast natural-RNA-self-LLPS data the project most needs **barely exists**, consistent with the v5 memory finding.

**Bottom line:** This sweep adds perhaps **~5–6 genuinely independent, high-value, non-yeast wet-lab sources** (~25–35 truly novel mineable sequences after dedup, heavily weighted toward G4/aptamer/designed-RNA mechanisms and matched negatives) plus useful feature signals and hard-negative enrichments — but it confirms the corpus is approaching the ceiling of what protein-free RNA-only-LLPS literature can supply. The remaining frontier is methodological (organism-stratified CV, mechanism tagging, hard-negative weighting), not more mining. The literature does not contain a hidden reservoir of clean non-yeast natural-RNA positives; that reservoir has not been generated experimentally yet.
